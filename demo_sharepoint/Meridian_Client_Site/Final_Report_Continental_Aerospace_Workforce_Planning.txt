EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Meridian Consulting Group was engaged to develop a 5-year strategic workforce plan for Continental Aerospace Corp's engineering and manufacturing divisions. Based on our analysis of 6,200 current employees, projected business growth, and industry talent supply, we have identified critical skill gaps and recommend a targeted talent acquisition and development strategy.

KEY FINDINGS

1. Critical Talent Gap — Advanced Manufacturing
   Continental faces a shortage of 340 engineers qualified in additive manufacturing and composites by 2028.
   Current hiring velocity: 45 engineers/year
   Required velocity: 115 engineers/year
   Gap: 70 engineers/year over 4 years

2. Retention Risk — Top 15% Performers
   Attrition risk analysis identified 89 high-value employees (out of 6,200) with >70% probability of departure in the next 18 months.
   Estimated replacement cost: $2.3M per senior engineer
   Total risk exposure: $204.7M

3. Compensation Benchmarking
   Continental's total compensation package ranks in the 41st percentile vs. industry peer set.
   Recommended market adjustment: 8-12% increase in base salary for critical roles.
   Projected annual cost: $18.4M
   
4. Succession Planning Gaps
   Of 47 Vice President and above positions, only 12 have identified successors ready within 2 years. 
   18 VP roles have no succession candidate identified.

RECOMMENDATIONS
1. Establish Advanced Manufacturing Academy (investment: $12M over 3 years)
2. Deploy targeted retention packages for the 89 at-risk employees
3. Implement 12% compensation adjustment for top 3 critical job families
4. Accelerate succession planning with structured development programs for 35 high-potential employees
